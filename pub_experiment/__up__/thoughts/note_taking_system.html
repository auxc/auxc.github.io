Core principles

  1. Write for myself.
  2. Edit for others.
  3. Link pages into thoughts and stories.
  4. Fact check and reference everything external.
  5. Share wips and talk about it often. It's an important part of the iterative process.[1]

Inspirations

Writing style

I decided to not worry too much about consistency and indulge in writing in whatever style feels good at the given moment. Somehow I seem to alternate between academic tone and silly antics(see the opening paragraph of this page). This should be alright, at least for now - I'm getting back into the swing of things with writing. Having a little fun with it has the markings of play, which I know is important for me to keep doing it.

Sometimes I might want to publish things. In such cases I can tweak and edit the text to a satisfactory quality. Rules 1 and 2 apply - write for myself first, edit and refine for a purpose later.

There is an experimental angle to this as well - I've been thinking recently about novel experiences and their effects on coginition, such as the perception of time and memory.

Technology

I decided to develop my own suite of tools by extending a simple text editor.[1]
